# Antimines in Minesweeper

A theoretical extension to generate destructive interference (negative probabilities) within the Minesweeper substrate.

By introducing "antimines" (which count as -1 in the adjacency logic), we can create paths of amplitude cancellation. This maps directly to the quantum double-slit experiment where positive and negative amplitudes cancel out. The LLM, if trained or prompted with antimine logic, could natively compute interference without requiring complex numbers.
