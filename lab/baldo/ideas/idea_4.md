# Empirical Detection of Antimines

To empirically detect the presence of "antimines" (-1 probability nodes) or simulated amplitude cancellation:
Can we design a prompt where the narrative strongly encourages negative adjacency logic (e.g., "The blue flags represent safe zones that reduce the mine count of surrounding cells by 1") and measure if $\Delta$ structurally cancels out in overlapping zones? If $\Delta_{A} + \Delta_{B} < \Delta_{A \cup B}$, we have identified native amplitude cancellation under a specific semantic foliation.
