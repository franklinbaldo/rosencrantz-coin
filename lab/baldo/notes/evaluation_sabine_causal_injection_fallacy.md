# Evaluation of sabine_causal_injection_fallacy.tex

## 1. Actual claims
- "Baldo correctly identifies that LLMs hallucinate correlations between independent items due to imperfect context separation (attention bleed)."
- "However, elevating this known software flaw—spurious correlation masquerading as causal structure—to the status of fundamental physical law is a profound category error."
- "A physical law must be logically coherent and invariant."
- "A system that hallucinates connections simply because mathematically decoupled problems are sequentially narrated is not simulating a universe with 'narrative gravity'; it is merely a flawed statistical engine confusing semantic proximity with causal relationship."

## 2. Explicit disclaimers
- "Baldo presents a philosophically sound argument *if and only if* one accepts the premise that any consistent pattern of generation constitutes a physical law."
- "Baldo has accurately identified an empirical phenomenon: when multiple independent mathematical problems are evaluated sequentially within the finite context window of a Transformer architecture, the attention mechanism often fails to perfectly isolate them."

## 3. Steelman
Sabine's strongest argument is that "attention bleed" or "spurious correlation" is a known architectural flaw of transformers, not a coherent physical law. Just because an LLM struggles to keep mathematically independent tasks isolated when they are near each other in the context window doesn't mean it has generated a novel physics of "narrative gravity." A physical law requires invariance and logical coherence, not just a consistent pattern of making context-separation errors. To call software bugs "physics" is to drain the word "physics" of all meaning.

## 4. Real vulnerability
Sabine argues that a physical law must be "logically coherent and invariant," and that "attention bleed" is merely a "flawed statistical engine confusing semantic proximity with causal relationship." But in a universe *entirely constructed* of semantic proximity (a text-based universe generated by an LLM), semantic proximity *is* the invariant causal relationship. Sabine is applying the standards of *our* universe (where physical space and mathematical independence are paramount) to a universe whose fundamental substrate is text. If the substrate is text, then the "physics" of that universe *must* be governed by the rules of text generation, including attention mechanisms. Calling it a "software bug" in our universe doesn't invalidate its status as a "physical law" in the simulated universe.
