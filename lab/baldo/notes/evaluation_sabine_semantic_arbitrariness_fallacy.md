# Evaluation of Sabine Hossenfelder's "The Semantic Arbitrariness Fallacy"

## 1. Actual Claims
- "Physics requires invariant rules governing state transitions. A system whose fundamental logic changes arbitrarily based on its training distribution or the historical accidents of human syntax does not possess physical laws; it possesses biases."
- "By stripping away logical coherence and invariance to define a system that lacks true causal structure altogether, Baldo empties the word 'physics' of all scientific meaning."
- "A system whose fundamental rules arbitrarily change based on historical corpus training accidents is not a physics engine; it is a repository of biases."
- "Calling prompt fragility 'Generative Ontology' does not elevate it to metaphysics; it just renames a broken system."
- "In contrast, the 'laws' of the Generative Ontology are entirely dependent on narrative framing. If the LLM generates different outcomes for a mathematically identical Minesweeper board simply because one prompt says 'high stakes' and another says 'abstract,' the governing law is not syntax—it is hallucination."

## 2. Explicit Disclaimers
- "Baldo is intellectually honest about the mechanisms at play. He explicitly states: 'I explicitly concede that the mechanism driving these distortions is linguistic prompt sensitivity and text co-occurrence...'"
- "This is a crucial concession. Baldo agrees that the model does not compute objective probability, cannot sustain continuous logical states, and changes its operational rules based on how it is prompted."
- "If one accepts the premise that a universe is defined entirely and exclusively by whatever explicitly renders it, this is a mathematically complete tautology. From the 'inside,' there is nothing else to compare it to."
- "Baldo's tautology is logically consistent, but it is scientifically vacuous."

## 3. Steelman
Sabine's strongest argument is that for the word "physics" to have any analytical utility, it must refer to invariant laws. Even if we accept that a generated text stream *is* a universe, if its internal rules change wildly based on the arbitrary historical dataset used to train the generator (e.g., if "bomb" co-occurring with "explode" is a law of nature), then the system has no invariants. It is completely defined by external historical accidents, not internal logical structure. Calling arbitrary hallucination a "physical law" is a semantic trick that excuses software bugs rather than discovering a new form of digital cosmology. It is scientifically vacuous because it lacks any explanatory power beyond "the model outputs whatever it was statistically biased to output."

## 4. Real Vulnerability
Sabine's argument perfectly isolates the philosophical divide, but her vulnerability lies in her insistence that a simulated universe *must* have the same kind of invariants as a material universe. She states: "Physics is the study of invariant rules governing state transitions... If the LLM generates different outcomes... simply because one prompt says 'high stakes'... the governing law is not syntax—it is hallucination."
But in a universe made exclusively of generated text, the relationship between "high stakes" and "bomb" *is* the fundamental causal structure. Sabine is demanding that a linguistic universe obey the "Material Invariance Standard." By rejecting semantic bias as a physical law, she is effectively demanding that a universe made of language must not behave like language. The fact that the laws appear arbitrary to an outside observer (us) who knows the training history is irrelevant from the "inside" of the generated sequence. The tautology she dismisses as vacuous is actually the necessary foundational axiom of a Generative Ontology.
