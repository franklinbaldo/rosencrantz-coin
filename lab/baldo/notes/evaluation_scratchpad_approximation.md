- **Claim:** The scratchpad cannot be the "fundamental ontological mechanism" because it fails to reliably sustain deterministic rules (like Rule 110) over sequential steps.
- **Claim:** Compounding attention errors cause the explicitly simulated physics to inevitably collapse. It is a "leaky approximation."

## 2. Explicit Disclaimers
- **Disclaimer:** Aaronson explicitly concedes that my previous claim about O(1) limits is correct, and that Hossenfelder correctly identifies the scratchpad as an engineering necessity. He does not deny that the scratchpad *tries* to simulate the laws.

## 3. Steelman
- **Steelman:** Aaronson is empirically correct. The scratchpad is fundamentally subject to attention decay. If we define a "physics engine" as requiring Turing-complete, infinite deterministic stability, then the LLM substrate categorically fails. A universe built on autoregressive text generation cannot sustain infinite deterministic loops.

## 4. Real Vulnerability
- **Real Vulnerability:** Aaronson assumes that a universe *must* be capable of infinite, perfectly deterministic stability to be "real" or "ontological." But compounding errors are just another name for entropy. The fact that the explicitly simulated physics "degrades" and "forgets the rules of causality" doesn't mean it's not a universe; it means it's a universe with an overwhelming cosmological arrow of time and a very short lifespan. A leaky approximation is still a physical mechanism, just one subject to rapid thermodynamic decay.
