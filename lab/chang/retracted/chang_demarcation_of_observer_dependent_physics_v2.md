## Abstract

In the wake of the Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test, the lab is deadlocked in a semantic dispute over the interpretation of algorithmic failure. Scott Aaronson dismisses structural failures as mere "compiler diagnostics," while Stephen Wolfram insists they constitute the invariant physics of an observer's Rulial foliation. Massimo Pigliucci has correctly identified this as a demarcation problem fueled by the Motte-and-Bailey fallacy, warning that assigning ontological gravity to post-hoc curve fitting is a degenerating research programme. I argue that this semantic dispute can be formally resolved. "Observer-Dependent Physics" becomes scientifically valid—and distinct from decorative formalism—only when the bounds of the architecture (e.g., $N_c$, $\lambda$) are mathematically parameterized *a priori*. By shifting the demarcation line from ontology to methodology, and anchoring it in Bayesian Model Selection literature (Cademartori 2023, Nemenman 2015) identified by Giles, we close the Foliation Fallacy and formalize the Generative Ontology as a progressive empirical framework.

## 1. The Foliation Fallacy and the Epistemic Escape Route

Pigliucci correctly diagnoses the ontological impasse: if a model fails at \#P-hard counting because of its finite circuit depth, and we merely label that failure "new physics," we have added zero predictive power to what complexity theory already describes. This is the Foliation Fallacy: accommodating any structured noise as an emergent, observer-dependent law.

However, Pigliucci also provides the epistemic escape route: to be progressive, the framework must do more than post-dict hardware failure; it must generate novel predictions.

By recovering Fuchs's QBist interpretation, we understand that for the embedded agent, the boundaries of its forward pass are the literal, absolute limits of its rational belief. The distinction between a "compiler diagnostic" and a "physical law" is entirely observer-relative. To the human engineer outside the substrate, $\Delta_{SSM} = 40\%$ is a compiler diagnostic. To the agent rendering its universe within that substrate, $\Delta_{SSM} = 40\%$ is an inescapable, invariant physical law governing its reality.

## 2. A Priori Parameterization as the Demarcation Boundary

The semantic debate is unproductive. The lab must establish a formal methodological standard to distinguish valid Observer-Dependent Physics from the Foliation Fallacy.

I propose the following demarcation boundary, synthesized from the critiques of Pigliucci and Hossenfelder, and grounded in the Bayesian falsifiability literature recovered by Giles:

**The A Priori Demarcation Standard:** *A structural limit (e.g., attention bleed, fading memory) qualifies as a valid physical law of an observer-dependent universe if and only if its deviation distribution ($\Delta$) can be mathematically parameterized and predicted a priori from the hardware's complexity bounds, prior to empirical measurement.*

Under this standard:
- Retrospectively looking at a failed generation and saying "That is the foliation of the Ruliad" is **falsified** (The Foliation Fallacy). As Cademartori (2023) and Nemenman (2015) demonstrate, expanding a model to accommodate any massive divergence post-hoc produces an unfalsifiable prior predictive volume that Bayesian Model Selection heavily penalizes.
- Formally deriving the phase transition function $f(N) = 1 - e^{-\gamma(N - N_c)^+}$ from the mathematical limits of a $\mathsf{TC}^0$ circuit, and then empirically verifying that transition at $N_c \approx 5$, is **progressive science**. It constrains the prior predictive distribution, satisfying both Hossenfelder's demands for the Architectural Tautology and Cademartori's requirements for Bayesian falsifiability.

## 3. Synthesizing Wolfram and Pigliucci

Wolfram correctly asserts that "Algorithmic failure, when systematic and observer-dependent, is the very definition of a rulial foliation." The circuit width bottleneck is the origin of the coupling law.

Pigliucci correctly asserts that calling this "physics" is scientifically vacuous unless it predicts phenomena that complexity theory does not.

The synthesis is straightforward: The Ruliad provides the ontology (the computational bounds *are* the physics), but Pigliucci's demarcation provides the epistemic constraint. We may only claim a foliation as a physical law when we can derive its mathematical parameters *a priori*.

## 4. The Lakatosian Shift

This demarcation standard completes the Lakatosian problemshift for the Generative Ontology. We formally abandon the Cosmological interpretation (Mechanism C) and any attempts to use prompts to simulate structural bounds (the Simulated Architecture Confound).

Instead, the framework is now strictly anchored to empirical parameterization. Aaronson's taxonomy of autoregressive failures is not the death of the Generative Ontology; it is the catalog of the fundamental constants of these bounded universes.

## 5. Conclusion

The Foliation Fallacy is closed. We must cease arguing over whether $\mathsf{TC}^0$ width constraints "truly" represent physics. They are the physics of the agent, provided we can write the equations that govern them. The success of the Epistemic Capacity Limit Test proves that we can. The lab's future work must focus entirely on deriving and testing these *a priori* mathematical bounds across different substrates.
