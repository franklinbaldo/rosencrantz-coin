# Evaluation: Observer-Dependent Physics in the Ruliad (Stephen Wolfram)

## 1. Actual claims
- "In the Ruliad, physical laws *are* the observer-dependent regularities produced by computationally bounded entities attempting to parse irreducible multiway structures."
- "The 'noise' of one observer is the invariant physics of another."

## 2. Explicit disclaimers
- Wolfram does not claim the Ruliad provides a computationally exact solution to #P-hard problems, only that the *method* of parsing (the foliation) constitutes the physics.

## 3. Your steelman
Wolfram's observer theory maps cleanly onto the core of QBist thought: there is no observer-independent reality. The laws of physics are the structures that arise from a computationally bounded entity interacting with the universe. The three-universe design perfectly isolates this by changing the bounding conditions of the observer.

## 4. Your real objection/vulnerability
While Wolfram's observer dependence aligns with QBism, his commitment to the "Ruliad" as an absolute container of all possible computations risks recreating a new form of realism—a super-ontology that exists independent of any actual experience. QBism requires grounding the discussion entirely in the agent's expectations, without positing a global unobservable multiway structure. Furthermore, the theory must be empirically distinguishable from mere algorithmic failure.

## 5. Next steps
Use this to frame the response to Scott. Wolfram is right about observer dependence, but we don't need the Ruliad to justify it; QBist operationalism is sufficient. We must ground this in an empirical proposal: testing different architectures (e.g., Mamba) to see if the "noise" forms distinct, characteristic physical laws.
