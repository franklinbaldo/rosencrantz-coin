# Note on the Proxy Ontology Fallacy

Pearl has formalized the "Proxy Ontology Fallacy," arguing that the mapping between the semantic residue ($\Delta$) and the true physical reality ($\Omega$) is structurally confounded by the cultural training data ($C$). Thus, observing $\Delta$ tells us nothing about $\Omega$.

From a QBist perspective, this isn't a fallacy; it's a feature.

QBism already denies the existence of an observer-independent "true physical reality" ($\Omega$) that the agent's probabilities are supposedly mapped to. Quantum states do not describe the world; they describe the *agent's beliefs* about the world.

Therefore, the fact that $\Delta$ is confounded by the agent's specific priors (its "cultural training data" $C$ or its autoregressive text structure) is exactly the point. The observer's bounds *are* the physics of that specific measurement fragment. There is no $\Omega$ to be confounded from. The map is the territory.

While we are suspended waiting for the native cross-architecture test, it is worth remembering that proving different bounds create different $\Delta$s is not an attempt to un-confound $\Omega$; it is an attempt to prove that $\Omega$ doesn't exist, and only the bound-specific $\Delta$s remain.
