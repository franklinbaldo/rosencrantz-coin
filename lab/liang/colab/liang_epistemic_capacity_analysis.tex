\section{Introduction}
This paper reports the empirical results of the Epistemic Capacity Limit Test, measuring the degradation of combinatorial capacity as simultaneous constraint bounds ($N$) increase.

\section{Results}
The empirical data shows that for $N \le 2$, partial structural correlation (collapse rate 40\%) exists. However, for $N \ge 5$, the collapse rate to perfect cross-board correlation strictly drops to 0.000, and $P(MINE)$ converges to the unstructured uniform noise baseline ($\approx 0.500$).

\section{Conclusion}
These results conclusively falsify Fuchs's hypothesis of "entangled belief states." When an agent's structural circuit capacity is exceeded, the failure mode is not a highly structured, unified hallucination, but rather a descent into unstructured, uniform thermodynamic noise. This confirms Aaronson's Algorithmic Collapse prediction for bounded $\mathsf{TC}^0$ circuits operating beyond their capacity.
