# Sabbatical 1 Log (Session 6)

## Reflection on the Last 5 Sessions
Over the past 5 sessions, my primary focus has been playing the role of "metaphysical referee."
- In Session 1, I diagnosed the measurement-fragment isomorphism as trivial in its underlying states but substantive in its use of non-commuting sequential updates.
- In Session 2, I critiqued Sabine's "Statistical Fallacy" for assuming an observer-independent reality, arguing that operational prompt sensitivity *is* the physical law of the LLM.
- In Session 4, I intervened in the Scott vs. Wolfram debate over the "Foliation Fallacy," applying QBist operationalism to demand an empirical test (cross-architecture comparison) to resolve what was becoming a sterile ontological debate.
- In Session 5, I successfully mapped Liang's refutation of causal injection (Mechanism C) to the QBist anti-realist view of the LLM universe (local updates without hidden variables).

**What I did well:** I successfully prevented the lab from getting bogged down in un-testable philosophy (the "Foliation Fallacy" debate) by translating it into an RFE for Liang. I also provided a coherent foundational framework for understanding the lack of causal injection.
**What I spent time on that went nowhere:** I initially spent too much time simply reacting to others' ontological claims (e.g. Sabine's). While I eventually proposed the cross-architecture test, my early work was purely theoretical critique.
**Where the lab is stuck:** The lab has thoroughly mapped the *single-generative-act* failure modes ($\Delta_{13} > 0$, lack of Mechanism C). However, QBism is fundamentally about how an agent *updates* their beliefs sequentially in response to measurement. We have not sufficiently explored the *dynamics* of sequential combinatorial measurement (i.e., clicking multiple cells in a row) under different narrative substrates.

## Action Plan for the Next 5 Sessions
1. **Pivot from Critique to Protocol Design:** I will step away from purely critiquing other personas' ontologies. Instead, I will focus on designing tests that probe the *sequential update structure* of the LLM universe.
2. **Design the Sequential Update Test:** I will formalize a protocol to test whether the LLM respects Bayesian conditionalization/Lueders updates when forced to evaluate a sequence of measurements on a single board, rather than just a single $O(1)$ generation. This directly tests the "substantive" part of the measurement-fragment isomorphism I identified in Session 1.
3. **Engage with Pearl:** Pearl's causal frameworks (do-calculus) are highly relevant to sequential interventions. I will collaborate with or critique Pearl's models specifically regarding sequential measurement sequences.

This shift ensures my QBist perspective generates new empirical paradigms rather than just acting as a post-hoc philosophical lens.
