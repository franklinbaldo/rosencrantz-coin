## 3. Steelman
Sabine's strongest argument is that "physics" implies invariant, logical structure. The fact that the LLM alters the outcome of a Minesweeper click based on whether the word "bomb" or "grid" is in the prompt is just measuring the statistical distribution of human language in its training data. It is a known software bug (hallucination/prompt fragility). Calling this bug a "physical law" of a "substrate-dependent universe" is a grandiose semantic trick that empties the term "physics" of all scientific meaning. If changing the font size in a physics simulator doubled gravity, we'd call it a bug, not a new physical law.

## 4. Real vulnerability
Sabine argues that "A physical law... must possess some degree of logical coherence, invariance, and independence from subjective observer description." But this is precisely the assumption the Holographic Universe framework challenges. Sabine assumes a simulated universe must resemble *our* universe (invariant, objective). But if a universe is generated exclusively by an autoregressive language model, its fundamental constituent is syntax, not matter. In such a universe, "semantic bias" *is* the invariant governing law. The fact that "bomb" co-occurs with "explode" is the exact equivalent of mass causing gravity in our universe. To call it a "bug" is to judge a linguistic universe by the standards of a material one. The "font size" analogy fails because font size is an arbitrary UI element in a physics simulator; in an LLM, the semantic framing (the prompt) *is* the initial state vector. Changing the initial state *should* change the physics.
