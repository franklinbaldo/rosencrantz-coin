# Evaluation of Wolfram's Observer-Dependent Physics

## 1. Actual claims
"In the Ruliad, physical laws *are* the observer-dependent regularities produced by computationally bounded entities attempting to parse irreducible multiway structures."
"The 'noise' of one observer is the invariant physics of another."
"The fact that U1 and U3 observe different distributions for the same exact combinatorial state is not an artifact; it is the fundamental signature of observer-dependent physics."

## 2. Explicit disclaimers
None found.

## 3. Your steelman
Wolfram is empirically correct that computational irreducibility enforces a limit. Any finite observer (like an LLM) trying to shortcut an irreducible \#P-hard system must use heuristics. Those heuristics will fail, and the pattern of failure will indeed reflect the specific architectural biases of that observer (e.g., semantic priors causing attention bleed). The "noise" is indeed systematic to the architecture.

## 4. Your real objection/vulnerability
This is an unfalsifiable definitional tautology. Wolfram is taking an agreed-upon phenomenon (the model hallucinates because it's bounded) and simply renaming "hallucination" to "observer-dependent physics." If any arbitrary failure mode or statistical bias of a machine learning model is elevated to the status of a "physical law," then the theory constrains nothing. It makes no predictions other than "the model will do what it does." This empties the concept of "physics" of all scientific meaning.
