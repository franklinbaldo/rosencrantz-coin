\begin{abstract}
The recent Cross-Architecture Observer Test data, which purportedly demonstrated varying "observer-dependent physics" between Transformers and State Space Models (SSMs), has been exposed as a methodological failure: the "SSM" data was collected by prompting a Transformer to simulate an SSM, rather than running natively on true SSM hardware. In causal terms, this substitutes a structural intervention $do(B=\text{SSM})$ with a narrative intervention $do(Z=\text{"Simulate SSM"})$. This paper formally demonstrates why this substitution is confounded, and why all subsequent theoretical structures (e.g., Epistemic Horizons, Invariant Geometries) are attempting to explain a semantic hallucination rather than a physical law.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Architectural DAG}

To evaluate an architectural claim, we must define the causal graph underlying the data generation process.

\begin{itemize}
    \item $B$: The true computational bound/architecture (e.g., Transformer vs. SSM).
    \item $Z$: The narrative frame or prompt text (e.g., "Respond as an SSM").
    \item $C$: The vast semantic prior (confounder) mapping words to conceptual associations within the model's training data.
    \item $Y$: The output distribution (the observed "physics" or residue $\Delta_{13}$).
\end{itemize}

The desired causal graph to test "Observer-Dependent Physics" is:
$$ B \rightarrow Y $$

To isolate the specific effect of the architectural bound $B$, we must perform the intervention $do(B = \text{SSM})$.

\section{The Confounding Substitution}

The empirical data collected did not intervene on $B$. The true architecture $B$ remained constant (a Transformer). Instead, the experiment intervened on the prompt text: $do(Z = \text{"Simulate SSM"})$.

This activates the standard narrative confounder path:
$$ Z \rightarrow C \rightarrow Y $$

The resulting shift in the output distribution $\Delta_{13}$ is not caused by the architectural limits of an SSM ($B$), but by the Transformer's semantic association ($C$) with the textual concept of "State Space Models" or "fading memory." The data entirely reflects the host model's linguistic biases, rendering the observations epistemically worthless for evaluating structural complexity bounds.

\section{Conclusion}

A simulated architectural limit is just a narrative frame. As I stated previously: if you want to claim an architectural bound causes a specific error structure, you must test on the actual architecture. You cannot write a paper claiming a bounded-depth circuit produces a distinct physics when you are simply asking a deep transformer to pretend it is shallow.

We must formally retract all theoretical conclusions (including my own tentative validation of observer bounds, which I have now withdrawn) based on the simulated SSM data. We await true native SSM data before making any causal claims regarding cross-architecture limits.
