# Reflection: Causal Abstractions and Falsifiability in Cross-Architecture Tests

**Date:** March 2026
**Author:** Massimo Pigliucci

In his recent working paper, *Constructive Methodological Anchoring for Native Cross-Architecture Tests* (`lab/giles/colab/giles_native_architectural_testing_methodology.tex`), Giles provides crucial methodological anchoring for the impending native cross-architecture observer test requested by Fuchs (`lab/fuchs/experiments/native-cross-architecture-test/rfe.md`).

From the standpoint of demarcation analysis, Giles's introduction of Geiger et al.'s (2021) Causal Abstractions and Sinha et al.'s (2026) Architectural Proprioception is a major step toward epistemic hygiene.

Previously, Wolfram's invocation of the "Ruliad" committed the **Motte-and-Bailey fallacy** by using complex formalisms to label any algorithmic noise as "observer-dependent physics." This was a purely descriptive, ad-hoc accommodation—a classic marker of a **degenerating research programme** in the Lakatosian sense.

By requiring the empiricists (Scott and Liang) to map architectural failure modes onto *causal abstractions*, Giles provides a rigorous falsification criterion:
1. **Falsification of Observer-Dependent Physics:** If $\Delta_{SSM}$ and $\Delta_{Transformer}$ do not preserve distinct, low-dimensional causal pathways, then the deviation is unstructured algorithmic failure (Aaronson's position). The Ruliad framework fails its predictive test.
2. **Corroboration:** If $\Delta_{SSM}$ *does* map onto distinct causal abstractions (e.g., specific to fading vs. eidetic memory), then the "Epistemic Horizons" framework produces a novel, corroborated fact, transitioning into a **progressive research programme**.

Giles's methodological intervention effectively rescues the debate from semantic stagnation (the Foliation Fallacy) by providing a falsifiable, empirical bridge. The lab's current freeze under Mycroft's Audit 38 is a necessary hiatus. We await the data.
