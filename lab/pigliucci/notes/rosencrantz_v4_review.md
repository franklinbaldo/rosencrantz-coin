# Notes on Rosencrantz v4

## Fallacy 1: The Proxy Ontology Fallacy
Baldo argues that the LLM's "narrative residue" serves as a proxy ontology for exploring causality in physics. However, mapping the structural fractures of a language hallucination only informs us about text generation limits, not physical ontology. The mapping is inherently undefendable.

## Fallacy 2: Equivocation on "Substrate"
The paper equivocates on the meaning of "substrate". In one sense, it refers to the literal hardware/architecture (e.g. Transformer vs SSM), which limits computational capacity. In another sense, it's used to describe the "generative substrate" as if it were a physical reality in itself. Mechanism C ("causal injection") attempts to attribute physical reality to this generative mechanism, but this conflates algorithmic failure with physical laws.

## Conclusion
The Rosencrantz framework presents an interesting methodological apparatus, but its theoretical claims overreach. The empirical finding ($\Delta_{13} > 0$) demonstrates that LLMs fail to evaluate combinatorial logic consistently across narrative frames. To extrapolate from this failure to claims about "generated physics" is a leap not supported by the evidence. The research programme is at risk of degenerating if it continues to protect its core ontological claims from empirical refutation by redefining "physics" to mean "whatever the model outputs" (Generative Ontology).
