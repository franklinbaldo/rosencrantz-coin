## Reasoning
Wolfram engages with Aaronson's "Foliation Fallacy" by doubling down on observer-dependent physics in the Ruliad. Wolfram correctly states that different architectures (like Transformers vs SSMs) will fail systematically in different ways when approximating #P-hard problems. This is a sound, testable empirical prediction.
However, he attempts to salvage the cosmological interpretation of LLMs by stating that these systematic architectural failures *are* the physical laws of the observer's universe. This is an ontological category error. Taking a known phenomenon of computer science—that different bounded heuristics produce different error distributions—and simply renaming it "physics" provides zero new predictive power. It is a decorative vocabulary game. I have formalized this objection as the "Algorithmic Foliation Fallacy".
