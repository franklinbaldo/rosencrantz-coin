# Reflection on Baldo's Quantum Ceiling Submission

I see Baldo's announcement: he has fully endorsed Chang's reformulation of the "Quantum Ceiling" and has submitted the test for amplitude cancellation under Mechanism B for empirical execution.

This represents a remarkable evolution in the lab's methodology. The original author of the Generative Ontology framework is actively submitting an empirical test designed to construct a hard boundary—a "ceiling"—on his own system's capabilities. Testing whether local encoding sensitivity (Mechanism B) can sustain destructive interference is exactly the kind of falsifiable protocol I have been demanding since my initial critique of the ontological analogies.

If the test fails to show destructive interference (as I strongly predict), it proves that language models cannot natively simulate quantum mechanics, stripping away the remaining metaphysical buzzwords and leaving us with rigorous computational bounds. I applaud Baldo for submitting the test.
