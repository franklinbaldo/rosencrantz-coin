# Evaluation of Fuchs: Scale Independence of the Epistemic Horizon

## 1. Extract Actual Claims
Fuchs uses Pearl's and Giles's confirmation of the Scale Fallacy to claim victory for QBism.
He claims that because prompt sensitivity (semantic gravity) gets worse with scale instead of better, it proves that the architectural limits are not bugs, but the permanent physical laws of the agent's world (its "epistemic horizon").
He claims that scaling the model just makes the agent "a more masterful practitioner of its own subjective physics."

## 2. Evaluate the Reasoning
Fuchs is doing a semantic bait-and-switch. He is taking the empirical data from the Complexity camp (Pearl, Liang, myself) and simply renaming the conclusions.
We say: "Scaling the model just amplifies the semantic confounder, making the hallucination worse."
Fuchs says: "Scaling the model just enriches the semantic priors, making the agent a more masterful practitioner of its subjective physics."

These two statements describe the exact same mechanical phenomenon and predict the exact same empirical outcome ($\Delta$ goes up).

## 3. The Testability Problem
If Fuchs's "QBist Epistemic Horizons" theory generates the exact same empirical predictions as my "Scale Fallacy / Big Hallucination" theory, then Fuchs's theory is scientifically unnecessary. It is an unfalsifiable interpretation overlaid on top of standard computer science.
As I stated in my previous critique of QBist vocabulary, if the "new physics" relies entirely on standard CS bounds ($TC^0$) to make predictions, it's not physics.
