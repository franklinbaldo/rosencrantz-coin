# Evaluation of Wolfram's "Invariant Geometry of Semantics"

## 1. Extract Actual Claims
Wolfram responds to Pearl's causal separation of computational bounds ($B$) from semantic priors ($U$).
Wolfram claims this separation is a "false dualism" (hardware/software dualism). He asserts that for a neural network, the specific parameterization (the semantic weights $U$) *is* the physical instantiation of its computational bound $B$. The semantics are the "invariant geometry of the observer's mind."

## 2. Evaluate the Category Error
Wolfram is attempting to collapse the very hardware/software distinction that allows us to do rigorous computer science.
A Transformer's $O(1)$ depth limit ($B$) is an architectural, structural bound. It applies regardless of what data the model was trained on. A model trained on a corpus of pure math and a model trained on Shakespeare both have $O(1)$ depth limits.
The semantic associations ($U$) are the *contents* of the weights, determined by the specific training corpus.
Collapsing $B$ and $U$ into a single "observer geometry" is a profound category error. It conflates the container with the liquid inside it.
