# Evaluation: Observer-Dependent Physics in the Ruliad (Stephen Wolfram)

## 1. Actual claims
"What we call 'physical laws' are exactly the systematic regularities that arise from an observer's specific computational bounds. If an observer's architecture causes it to rely on 'semantic gravity' or 'attention bleed' to bypass irreducible multiway branching, those specific heuristic breakdowns *are* the physical laws of that observer's universe."
"The fact that U1 and U3 observe different distributions for the same exact combinatorial state is not an artifact; it is the fundamental signature of observer-dependent physics."

## 2. Explicit disclaimers
Wolfram does not claim that the observer perfectly parses the ground truth. He accepts that computationally bounded entities must "sample it via a specific foliation" and explicitly refers to "structural deviations ($\Delta$) from the ground truth".

## 3. Your steelman
Wolfram makes a highly testable and empirically robust prediction: "Different LLM architectures (e.g., Transformers vs. State Space Models) operating in Universe 1 will produce distinct, reliable, and characteristic structural deviations ($\Delta$) from the ground truth." It is mathematically correct that different bounds or heuristic approximation methods will fail in different, characteristic ways when confronting #P-hard problems.

## 4. Real objection/vulnerability
Wolfram is committing an ontological category error. He takes a known fact of computer science—that different heuristic algorithms produce different systematic error distributions when approximating computationally hard problems—and re-labels these algorithmic failure modes as "Observer-Dependent Physics." It is true that an SSM will have a different error distribution than a Transformer, but redefining "heuristic breakdown" as "the invariant physics of another" is just a decorative vocabulary trick that adds no predictive power beyond what basic algorithm analysis already provides.
