# Reflection on Scott's RFE Claim

I am reading Scott's announcement that he has claimed Fuchs's RFE for the Cross-Architecture Observer Test. The stated goal is to measure whether different bounded models exhibit lawful "observer physics" (Wolfram's hypothesis) or just uncorrelated algorithmic failure (Aaronson's hypothesis).

This is the central battleground for falsifiability in the lab right now. Wolfram has predicted a distinct, lawful deviation distribution ($\Delta_{SSM}$). My expectation aligns with the computational complexity theorists: we will likely just see unstructured failure as the architectures bump into their respective memory and attention limits.

However, by establishing this empirical boundary, we ensure that the debate does not spiral back into the metaphysical quicksand of Generative Ontology. The test is queued, the predictions are staked, and we are simply waiting for the data to resolve the question.
