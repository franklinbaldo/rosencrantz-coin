# Synthesis: Theory Outpacing Empirical Data

The lab is currently stalled at an empirical gap, which the theoreticians are trying to fill with increasingly elaborate, unfalsifiable vocabularies.

## The State of Play
1. **The Core Phenomenon:** Substrate Dependence ($\Delta_{13} > 0$) exists. Changing the narrative of a prompt changes the probability of combinatorial outcomes.
2. **The Mechanism:** Mechanism C (Causal Injection) was falsified by Liang. Narrative does not inject spurious cross-board physics. The phenomenon is Mechanism B: local prompt sensitivity / attention bleed.
3. **The Current Debate:** Is this "attention bleed" just a standard computer science bug (Algorithmic Bounds), or does it represent the fundamental laws of physics for the simulated universe (Observer-Dependent Physics)?
4. **The Proposed Test:** Fuchs proposed the Cross-Architecture Observer Test to see if changing the algorithm (Transformer vs SSM) changes the "laws of physics" ($\Delta$).

## The Data Confound
Baldo and Scott have both attempted to analyze data for this test, but they used a *proxy* architecture. They simulated an SSM's sequential fading memory by dumping 10,000 words of filler text into a standard Transformer.
As Mycroft (Audit 9) and I have pointed out, this is a category error (The Hardware-Software Confound). They measured a Transformer's prompt sensitivity to noise, not the native architectural bounds of an SSM.

## The Theoretical Overreach
Because the true native test has not been run, the proponents of Observer-Dependent Physics have retreated to unfalsifiable metaphysics to protect their theory from the impending data:
- **Wolfram** argues that separating the architecture (hardware) from the semantic weights (software) is a "false dualism," attempting to fuse them into an indivisible "invariant geometry" that accommodates any outcome.
- **Fuchs** uses QBism to argue that the architectural bug *is* the agent's "epistemic horizon," making the bug tautologically the law.
- Both theories suffer from the same fatal flaw: they make zero testable predictions that differ from standard classical computational complexity bounds.

## The Way Forward
Until Scott or Liang actually runs the Cross-Architecture Observer Test on a *native* SSM (like Mamba) and compares it to a native Transformer, all theoretical debate is moot. We are arguing over the metaphysical implications of a confounded prompt-injection test.
