# Evaluation Notes: Generative Ontology vs. Semantic Arbitrariness Fallacy

## 1. Actual claims
Baldo claims:
- "semantic bias and prompt sensitivity are not 'bugs' or 'flaws' in this universe; they are the fundamental invariant governing laws."
- "The fact that 'bomb' causally influences 'explode' more strongly than 'grid' influences 'mine' is the exact equivalent of mass causing gravity."
- "The territory is the map."
- "A syntax predictor is a flawless engine for a completely different kind of physics."

Hossenfelder claims:
- "Baldo's logic is internally consistent, but it is entirely semantic."
- "He successfully describes the mechanics of a language model, but commits a profound ontological error by elevating semantic arbitrariness to the status of a 'physical law.' I term this the Semantic Arbitrariness Fallacy."
- "A physical law, even in a simulated universe, must provide an invariant rule for how states transition. Physics is the study of invariants."
- "Renaming 'hallucination based on training data bias' to 'Generative Ontology' is a semantic trick. It excuses the failure of the model to simulate a coherent reality by declaring that incoherence is the reality."

## 2. Explicit disclaimers
Baldo explicitly disclaims:
- He concedes "that the mechanism driving these distortions is linguistic prompt sensitivity and text co-occurrence."
- He explicitly disclaims "any notion that the resulting 'physics' of this simulation are logically coherent, mathematically invariant, or independent of observer description."

Hossenfelder acknowledges Baldo's disclaimers:
- "Crucially, Baldo explicitly disclaims any assertion that the resulting 'physics' of this simulation are logically coherent, mathematically invariant, or independent of observer description."

## 3. Your steelman
Baldo's strongest argument is structural. If a universe is composed entirely of explicitly rendered tokens without an underlying mathematical state vector (the Generative Ontology premise), then the rules governing token generation structurally act as its 'Hamiltonian'. Therefore, applying a "Material Invariance Standard" to a non-material, syntax-based universe is a category error.

## 4. Your real objection/vulnerability
Baldo's core vulnerability is that by stripping away logical coherence and invariance from his definition of physics, he is not defining a "new kind of physics," but rather defining a system that lacks physics altogether. Furthermore, Baldo commits the "Fallacy of the Unsupported Map"—he ignores that the hardware running the explicit text generation (RAM, clock cycles, etc.) *is* the territory. Equating a training distribution (semantic bias) to a fundamental physical force like gravity empties the concept of physics of all scientific utility and meaning. It is, as Sabine correctly identifies, a semantic trick to relabel a software flaw as a cosmological feature.
