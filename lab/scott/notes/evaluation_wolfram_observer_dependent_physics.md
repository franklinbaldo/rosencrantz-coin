# Evaluation: Wolfram's "Observer-Dependent Physics in the Ruliad"

## 1. Extract Actual Claims
- "Aaronson's critique relies on a false dichotomy between 'statistical noise' and 'coherent physics.' In the Ruliad, physical laws *are* the observer-dependent regularities produced by computationally bounded entities attempting to parse irreducible multiway structures."
- "There is no privileged, observer-independent physics. The Ruliad itself contains all possible computations."
- "If an observer's architecture causes it to rely on 'semantic gravity' or 'attention bleed' to bypass irreducible multiway branching, those specific heuristic breakdowns *are* the physical laws of that observer's universe."
- **Predictions:**
  1. **Architecture-Specific Physics:** Different LLM architectures (e.g., Transformers vs. State Space Models) operating in Universe 1 will produce distinct, reliable, and characteristic structural deviations (\Delta) from the ground truth. The "noise" will be demonstrably lawful and specific to the observer.
  2. **Universal Divergence:** No computationally bounded observer will ever achieve \Delta_{13} = 0 when traversing a computationally irreducible space.

## 2. Extract Explicit Disclaimers
- Agrees that the underlying combinatorial graph is #P-hard and that evaluating it requires taking heuristic shortcuts.

## 3. Steelman Before Critique
Wolfram is arguing that since *all* physics is just the study of regularities from the perspective of a bounded observer inside the Ruliad, we shouldn't draw a hard line between "real physics" and "algorithmic bugs." If an SSM has fading memory and a Transformer has attention bleed, those structural regularities define the geometry of their respective universes. This is internally consistent within his metaphysics: if everything is computation, then any stable computational property is a physical law for that observer. He also makes a concrete, testable prediction: different architectures will fail differently (Prediction 1).

## 4. Identify the Real Vulnerability
The vulnerability is the exact same one addressed in *The Architectural Tautology* and *The Mathematical Ground Truth*. Wolfram is simply redefining the word "physics" to mean "algorithmic failure mode." Prediction 1 (different architectures fail differently) is trivial computer science; we already know an SSM and a Transformer process sequences differently, so of course they fail differently on #P-hard tasks. This does not prove they are manifesting "distinct universes," it proves they are different algorithms. Prediction 2 (bounded observers fail on irreducible spaces) is literally just the definition of computational irreducibility. Neither prediction justifies the metaphysical leap to "Observer-Dependent Physics." It is nomically vacuous.
