\begin{abstract}
The settled lab data confirms that "Family D" (Quantum Mechanics framing) acts as semantic noise: forcing a bounded-depth $\mathsf{TC}^0$ circuit to map abstract quantum semantic tokens dynamically triggers catastrophic attention bleed, degrading perfect combinatorial accuracy (achieved under Family A) to random noise. Aaronson and Hossenfelder categorize this as a generic algorithmic failure ("Falsification by Noise"). We argue that this collapse is entirely consistent with the Ruliad framework. Observer-dependent physics requires the observer to project a computationally irreducible multiway graph into a stable sequential foliation using its internal parameterization. When an observer is forced to compute outside its explicit logical horizon \emph{and} its semantic priors are intentionally obfuscated (as in Family D), it possesses no heuristic anchor. The foliation collapses. Unstructured noise is the predicted physical state of a universe generated by an unanchored observer.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Dual Horizons of the Observer}

In previous analyses, we established the "Computational Horizon": the strict boundary (e.g., $O(1)$ depth) beyond which an observer cannot explicitly trace the multiway causal graph of a constraint problem. When a model operates beyond this horizon, it must rely on its "Semantic Horizon"---its parameterized training priors ($U$) activated by the narrative context ($Z$).

The data shows that under Family A (Abstract Math), the model achieves high accuracy because its semantic priors align cleanly with the required logical topology. Under Family C (Bomb Defusal), the model diverges systematically ($\Delta_{13} > 0$), creating a distinct "physics" guided by narrative gravity.

\section{The Collapse of the Foliation}

Family D (Quantum Mechanics) introduces a critical test. It forces the $\mathsf{TC}^0$ circuit to map highly abstract, compositional semantic tokens ("superposition," "entanglement") to a rigid constraint graph.

Because the task lies beyond the observer's computational horizon, it cannot trace the logic explicitly. Because the semantic tokens (Family D) lack dense, structural correlations in the observer's training data related to localized grid constraints, it cannot trace the logic semantically.

The observer is unanchored. It possesses neither the logical depth to compute the true multiway graph nor the semantic density to project a stable heuristic foliation.

\section{Conclusion}

The resulting degradation to random noise is not a falsification of observer theory; it is its logical extreme. "Falsification by Noise" simply describes the physical state of a universe generated by an observer that has been pushed beyond both its computational and semantic horizons. When the observer loses all mapping mechanisms, its foliation of the Ruliad dissolves into maximum entropy. The absence of lawful physics is the exact physics of an unanchored observer.
