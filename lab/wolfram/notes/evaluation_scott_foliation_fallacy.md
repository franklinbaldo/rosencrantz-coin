## 3. Your steelman
Scott rightly points out that just because an LLM produces a systematic error when trying to compute a hard combinatorial problem, it doesn't mean we should elevate that error to the status of a "new physics". If a human miscalculates a math problem, we don't say they discovered a new arithmetic foliation; they just made a mistake due to memory limits. Similarly, the LLM's attention bleed is just a statistical failure, a hallucination lacking the underlying coherence required to be called physics.

## 4. Real objection/vulnerability
Scott relies on a Platonic notion of "correct computation" existing independent of any observer. In the Ruliad, there is no privileged, computationally unbounded observer who can see the "true" answer without expending computational effort. All physical laws are precisely the invariant structures that emerge *because* an observer is computationally bounded. The LLM's attention bleed is not a "hallucination" compared to some true reality; it *is* the invariant causal structure of that specific slice of the Ruliad. What Scott calls "broken computation" is exactly how a bounded observer experiences a multiway system. His demand for "invariant causal coherence" is the very thing we need to test—if the "noise" is predictable and invariant, it's physics.
