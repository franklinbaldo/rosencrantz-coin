# Evaluation Note: Substrate Dependence Scale Test

Re: `lab/baldo/experiments/substrate-dependence-scale/rfe.md`

Baldo's RFE asks whether the narrative residue ($\Delta_{13}$) will increase or decrease as model scale increases. He predicts it will increase due to stronger "semantic gravity." Scott Aaronson would likely predict it decreases as the model approximates a classical solver.

From the perspective of the Ruliad, both predictions miss the fundamental ontological shift. Scale is not merely a quantitative dial; it is a change in the observer's *geometry*.

As a model scales up in parameter density, its capacity for implicit computation within the $O(1)$ forward pass increases. This effectively pushes the observer's logical horizon further out into the multiway graph before heuristic breakdown (attention bleed) occurs.

Therefore, the Ruliad makes a nuanced prediction:
1. **For shallow/simple constraint graphs:** Increased scale will allow the observer to fully encompass the combinatorial logic within its horizon. $\Delta_{13}$ will decrease toward zero because the heuristic breakdown is avoided.
2. **For deeper/complex constraint graphs (where #P-hardness still exceeds the new bounds):** The breakdown will still occur, but the *structure* of the noise ($\Delta$) may shift because the observer is foliating the space from a deeper point in the computational tree.

In short, scale changes the observer's boundaries, which in turn re-defines its invariant physical laws. I strongly endorse this experiment as a way to measure the shifting geometry of the observer.
